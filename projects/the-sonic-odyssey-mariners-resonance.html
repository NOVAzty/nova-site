About the Project

Why Do We Set Sail?

Tides rise and fall, clouds shift without warning, and the body learns—breath by breath—to accommodate the unknown. Within this slight unease, memory and association are magnified, and individual perception occasionally overlaps with generational echoes, like a rope that knots and loosens in turn.

The Sonic Odyssey: Mariners' Resonance seeks to construct an artistic field where sonic experimentation and conceptual depth converge. The audience will be invited to participate in the voyage together. Drawing inspiration from the Ming-era navigation manual Shunfeng Xiangsong (Voyage with a Tail Wind), the sea here becomes a metaphor: civilization persists in its flow, yet many destinations remain unfinished; and in the act of "seeking", we encounter the very incompleteness of the self.The cross cultural collaboration of musical instruments, such as Guqin’s continuous resonance and Gamelan’s interplay of collective structure and inner intuition mirror one another—an "outer seeking" and an "inner seeking" in dialogue. The elongated diction of Nanyin (a style of Chinese chamber music from the southern Chinese province of Fujian) resembles a fine thread, pulling the voyager back and forth between departure and return.

In this weaving of sea and wind, of seeking and answering, of departure and return, of the crowd and the self, every tide is a brief sojourn, and the act of moving on is a form of arrival itself.
