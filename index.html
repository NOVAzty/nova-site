About

Tianyi Zhang (NOVA) is graduated from Music Design and Production in the Department of Music Engineering at Shanghai Conservatory of Music. She focuses on the cross-border fusion of art, technology and philosophy. Her creative fields include experimental electronic music, new media theater, experimental video, performance art, noise and alternative music. She actively explores the cross-media expression of sound art and visual, dance, installation, etc., focusing on the subjective expression of sound, so as to form diversified narrative/non-narrative creations. Chaos and intermediate states are her current research interests.

She is also the founder of the interdisciplinary art collective M1∞ (M1 Infinitas), which seeks to break down disciplinary boundaries and promote cross-border collaborations and experimental creations through a variety of mediums and from the perspectives of different disciplines.

Her commissioned theater piece "Falling from the Sky", with composition and sound design, was performed at the China Shanghai International Arts Festival (2023), and was selected for the RAW Young Artists Support Program. In 2025, the electronic music theatre "The Sonic Odyssey: Mariners' Resonance" in which she served as artistic planning and composition, was once again selected for the RAW award. In addition, she was the sound and audio designer for the physical theater "The Secret Room" and the forum theater "Dog days". She participated in the workshop of renowned composer and Pulitzer Prize and Guggenheim Fellowship winner Prof. Du Yun, and composed the sound music for the original song project of Shanghai's century-old brand. Her works have been performed/exhibited at the Shanghai Music Hall, Shanghai Conservatory of Music, Shanghai Theater Academy, YUZ Museum Shanghai, Cheng Shifa Art Museum, HOW Art Museum, Tongji University, Liberal Town art space(Shanghai), etc., and have received widespread attention.

In addition, her academic research involves interdisciplinary practices such as sound art, performing art, cultural heritage, philosophy of technology and artificial intelligence. Her paper was once selected for the 12th Conference on Sound and Music Technology (CSMT 2025).
